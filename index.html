Methodology & caveats

What "robust" means: positive return on BOTH 4h and 1d EUR/USD data over a 120-day window. Filters out over-fitted single-timeframe configs.

Sample size caveat: top configs have n=3-8 trades on each timeframe over 120 days. That's not statistically significant on its own. The "robust" filter is doing more work than the absolute numbers — it tells you the config generalizes across timeframes, not that the returns are reliable estimates.

Multi-asset test: the 3 best (4h+1d) EUR/USD configs were re-run on 5 Kraken pairs. Mixed results — A_1to1 holds up on HBAR/BTC/XRP, but C_mid and B_1to5 are noisier. This suggests the divergence signal works but the SL/TP ratio is pair-dependent.

What this report does NOT do: live trading, OANDA order endpoints, walk-forward validation, regime tagging, or position sizing beyond fixed 2% per trade. The 18,432-config grid is a static snapshot from 2026-06-11 — no re-runs, no fresh data.
